# Governance

This document describes how the 8th Wall open source project is governed and how decisions are made.

---

## Principles

- **Openness** — decisions and discussions happen in public wherever possible  
- **Transparency** — the reasoning behind decisions is documented  
- **Inclusivity** — contributors of all experience levels are welcome  
- **Sustainability** — governance is designed to outlast any single organization or individual

---

## Technical Steering Committee (TSC)

The TSC is responsible for the technical direction of the project. It has final authority over:

- Roadmap and release planning  
- Accepting or rejecting significant contributions  
- Maintainer nominations and removals  
- Changes to governance itself

### Decision Making

The TSC aims to operate by consensus. When consensus cannot be reached, decisions are made by a simple majority vote of TSC members. 

Significant decisions (roadmap changes, governance amendments, maintainer changes) will be documented in GitHub Discussions with a minimum 7-day comment period before a vote is called.

---

## Maintainers

Maintainers have merge access to the repository and are responsible for day-to-day review and triage. All TSC members are maintainers. Additional maintainers may be nominated by the TSC.

### Becoming a Maintainer

Contributors who have demonstrated sustained, high-quality contributions may be nominated for maintainership by any existing TSC member. Nominations require a simple majority TSC vote to pass.

### Stepping Down

Maintainers who are inactive for more than 6 months may be moved to emeritus status by TSC vote.

---

## Contributors

Anyone who submits a PR, files an issue, or participates in GitHub Discussions is a contributor. There are no formal requirements to contribute. All contributions must comply with the and [Code of Conduct](https://github.com/8thwall/8thwall/blob/main/CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

---

## Project Stewardship

8th Wall is stewarded by [Niantic Spatial](https://www.nianticspatial.com/), who holds the project IP and trademark. Day-to-day technical governance of the project is the responsibility of the TSC, which operates independently of Niantic Spatial on all technical matters.

We are evaluating options for the long-term governance of the project, including the possible establishment of an independent foundation or partnership with a fiscal sponsor. This document will be updated periodically as needed to reflect any such changes.

---

## Amendments

Changes to this governance document require a two-thirds majority TSC vote and a 14-day public comment period in GitHub Discussions.
